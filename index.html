About Me

From Construction Site to Website, Always a Builder

Back in the middle of October 2022, while at work, I had an epiphany. After 3 years of working as a site engineer, I realized the things I like about my career wasn't entirely about my career. I would spend countless hours trying to make sense of thousands of data while doing a structural analysis of a structure, recalibrate things and redo the test. I enjoyed AutoCAD and would dive on tiny details that most of my peers would not even bother to notice when doing a floor plan. I've spent many a countless nights on Excel trying to create a spreadsheet that would automatically design a structure's footing base on my inputs. On the very same job when I had the epiphany, I learned how to fabricate, build, and install modular cabinets while barely knowing the industry existed a month prior. What all these things had in common? I built things with my own hand. I was the best version of myself when I get into zone building things with my own hands - something that is most likely not possible when working as a supervisor in a construction site. I realized I couldn't simply lead and let people build things, and though it was satisfying to be able to coordinate all resources and watch as a structure take form, nothing beats building things with your own hands. The sense of pride and ownership. I've always wanted to be a programmer, so the next working day, I submitted my resignation to the surprise of my colleagues, and enrolled myself to Zuitt Coding Bootcamp. 4 months later, I am still building - from Construction Site to Website.
